Hierarchy of Learning Objectives

To become a programmer, you need to have experiences that force you to move through a hierarchy of learning objectives. Doing so ensures that you can progress from simply recalling coding concepts to being able to develop your own original code. Bloom’s Taxonomy has six levels: Remember, Understand, Apply, Analyze, Evaluate, and Create.

Metacognition (Self-Assessment)

Usually defined as thinking about your thinking, it refers to the brain's processes used to plan, monitor, and assess your understanding and performance. Metacognition includes a critical awareness of a) one's thinking and learning and b) oneself as a thinker and learner.
